Beaches

The Western coast of Portugal’s Algarve has some of the best beaches in all of Europe, rimmed by high cliffs that drop dramatically down to the protected beaches. Feels eerily like Malibu, or the coast just north of Los Angeles, without any houses or city marring the vista.

Praia da Marinha Carvoeiro One of Portugal’s most iconic beaches, Praia da Marinha is framed by limestone cliffs and turquoise waters. Accessible via a steep staircase, this sheltered cove rewards the effort with golden sand, natural sea arches and perfect photo ops. It’s ideal for a peaceful swim, snorkeling or just marveling at nature’s artistry.
Praia do Camilo Lagos A winding staircase leads down to this picture-perfect beach nestled between towering orange cliffs. At low tide, you can walk through sea-carved tunnels into smaller coves, making it feel like your own private paradise. The waters here are calm and inviting — ideal for swimming or just soaking up the sun.
Praia da Guincho Cascais Rated as one of the best beaches in Portugal and on top ten beaches in the world, it’s a dream. Strong winds, perfect sand — a contrast to the rocky beaches of France and Italy — large waves and incredible sunsets, it just all adds up.
